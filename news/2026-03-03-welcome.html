Daily Briefing: Markets, MWC, and AI policy

March 3, 2026 · Briefing · 4 min read

Today’s takeaway: Energy volatility is back in the spotlight, markets are repricing risk, and the tech calendar is moving fast with major announcements at Mobile World Congress.

1) Markets: risk-off mood, energy jumps

Global markets turned defensive as the Middle East conflict escalated and investors focused on energy supply risks. Crude moved higher and gas prices also saw sharp moves in parts of Europe, pushing inflation concerns back into the conversation.

  • What we’re watching: energy price volatility, shipping disruptions, and how quickly risk assets stabilize.
  • Why it matters: higher energy costs can filter into inflation expectations and rate-path assumptions.

2) Europe: inflation data complicates the rate debate

February eurozone inflation came in higher than expected, with core and services components remaining sticky. This keeps the next set of central bank decisions firmly “data-dependent,” especially if energy prices remain elevated.

3) Tech: Mobile World Congress (MWC) highlights

MWC is underway in Barcelona, and the early theme is clear: AI-first hardware refreshes, thinner/lighter form factors, and more on-device intelligence across laptops, tablets, and mobile accessories.

  • Device refresh cycle: enterprise laptops and productivity devices are leaning into “AI PC” positioning.
  • Practical angle: buyers are asking for battery life, privacy, and offline capabilities—not just model size.

4) Policy: 2026 is shaping up as an “implementation year” for AI rules

Across the U.S. and abroad, more AI governance requirements are moving from headlines to compliance checklists. For teams shipping AI-enabled features, the operational work now looks like: documenting use cases, risk mapping, testing for bias/harms where relevant, and tightening vendor/third-party controls.
